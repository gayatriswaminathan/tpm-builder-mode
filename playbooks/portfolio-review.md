# Portfolio Review

> Patterns **#10** (ruthless prioritization + forced reallocation + sunset runbook), **#11** (self-check), **#12** (prune your own process).

## Cadence
Quarterly, plus any time a program's health changes sharply.

## 1. One-table snapshot

| Program | Phase | Health | Trend | Owner-hours/wk | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| <name> | <phase> | 🟢/🟡/🔴 | ↑/→/↓ | <n> | <verdict> |

**Verdict ∈ { DOUBLE-DOWN · MAINTAIN · MINIMAL · SUNSET }** — every program gets exactly one. "It depends" is not allowed (pattern #1).

## 2. The "4 hours" question
If you had only 4 focused hours this week across the whole portfolio, where would they go? Name the **top 3 priorities** and the **don't-touch** list.

## 3. Allocation by verdict
- **DOUBLE-DOWN** — disproportionate time; name the quarter's target metric + top 3 initiatives.
- **MAINTAIN** — steady, no major new investment.
- **MINIMAL** — life support (<2 hrs/wk); why it's not sunset (e.g. compounding value, low upkeep).
- **SUNSET** — run the runbook below.

## 4. Sunset runbook (reusable — deprecating is a checklist, not a fade)
1. Announce to stakeholders/users (T+0); give a wind-down window.
2. Freeze new intake/signups (T+0).
3. Export/handoff anything owed (T+window).
4. Shut down (T+end).
5. Archive artifacts and access.
6. Write the postmortem → feed into `templates/retro-learning-loop.md`.

## 5. Portfolio-level calls
Force 2–3 macro decisions (e.g. "stop starting new programs this quarter; concentrate on the two near their gate").

## 6. Self-check (required)
> Where am I likely being too optimistic or too pessimistic this quarter? <one paragraph — pattern #11.>

## 7. Prune the process (pattern #12)
Which of our own gates/rituals/templates demonstrably prevented a problem this quarter? Sunset the ones that only added overhead. Each ceremony must re-justify its place.
